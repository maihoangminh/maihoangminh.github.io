Comparison to other means of transportation

Experts agree that flying has never been safer. Arnold Barnett, a statistics professor at M.I.T., has been widely quoted as saying a person could fly every day for an average of 123,000 years before dying in a plane crash.

If You had a 0.000007 chance of being onboard any one of the crashed flights in 2014, or roughly 1 in 1.43 million. If you are a very frequent flier and you boarded 100 flights last year, your chances were about one in 14,300 that one of those flights would experience a fatality, although not necessarily that you would be one of those fatalities.

So why we scare of airplanes?

Psychology researchers, such as Paul Slovic and Baruch Fischhoff , have found that we most fear situations where we feel we have no control . So riding on your car or bikes feels safe, because you’re the one with the brakes. But airplane has been and always will be the safest transportation.
